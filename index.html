Croissant

The croissant is a pastry that originated in Austria as the kipferl, a crescent-shaped bread roll made from yeasted wheat dough. The kipferl's origins are shrouded in myth, but some say it was invented in the late 17th century to celebrate the Austrian victory over the Ottoman Empire at the siege of Vienna. The crescent shape of the kipferl represented the moon on the Ottoman flag
